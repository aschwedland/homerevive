Frequently Asked Questions

What’s the difference between a fence repair and a fence replacement?

The key difference between fence repair and fence replacement lies in the scope of the work and the condition of your existing fence. Fence repair focuses on fixing specific issues such as damaged panels, leaning posts, or broken gates, and is ideal when most of the fence is still in good shape. Repairs are quicker and more cost-effective, extending the life of your fence without the need for a full overhaul.

On the other hand, fence replacement involves removing the old fence and installing a completely new one. This option is best when the fence is beyond repair, such as when there is widespread damage, significant wear and tear, or structural issues that compromise its stability and appearance. A replacement offers the chance to choose new materials and designs, giving your property a fresh look while enhancing security and privacy.

If you're unsure whether you need a fence repair or replacement in Denver, CO, we offer free inspections to help determine the best solution based on the condition of your fence.

How do I maintain my fence after installation in Denver, CO?

Maintaining your fence after installation in Denver, CO is key to ensuring its longevity, especially with the area's varied weather conditions. For wood fences, regular maintenance includes applying a sealant or stain every 2-3 years to protect against moisture and UV damage. It’s also important to check for signs of rot or insect damage and replace any affected panels promptly.

For vinyl fences, maintenance is minimal—simply clean the surface with soap and water to remove dirt and debris. Metal fences, like wrought iron or aluminum, should be inspected for rust. If rust spots appear, sanding and repainting the affected areas will prevent further damage.

In Denver's climate, winter can be tough on fences, so after heavy snowfalls, clear away snow from the base to avoid moisture buildup. By keeping up with these simple tasks, your fence will remain sturdy and attractive for years to come.

How do I know if I need a fence repair or replacement?

Deciding between a fence repair and a fence replacement depends on the extent of the damage and the overall condition of your fence. If you notice minor issues like leaning posts, damaged panels, or a small amount of rot or rust, a repair is usually sufficient. These repairs can address the problem areas without the cost of a full replacement, and they extend the life of your fence.

However, if your fence has widespread damage, such as extensive rotting, warping, or rust, or if it’s showing signs of structural instability, it may be time for a full fence replacement. This is especially true if repairs would be frequent or costly, or if your fence no longer provides the necessary security or privacy. A replacement also allows you to upgrade to new materials and styles that better suit your property.

If you're unsure, we offer free assessments in Denver, CO to help you determine whether a fence repair or replacement is the best option for your situation.

What are the most durable fence materials for Denver’s climate?

In Denver’s climate, which experiences both harsh winters and dry summers, choosing durable fence materials is essential for long-term performance.
