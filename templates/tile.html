What is the difference between porcelain and ceramic tiles?

The main difference between porcelain and ceramic tiles lies in their composition and durability. Porcelain tiles are made from a denser clay and fired at higher temperatures, making them more water-resistant and durable than ceramic. This makes porcelain ideal for high-traffic areas and moisture-prone spaces like bathrooms and outdoor areas, as it can withstand heavy use and exposure to the elements.

On the other hand, ceramic tiles are slightly more porous and softer, which makes them easier to cut and install. They are a great option for indoor spaces like kitchens, backsplashes, or living areas where extreme durability isn’t as crucial. Ceramic tiles tend to be more affordable and come in a wide variety of colors and designs, making them a versatile choice for many home projects.

Both options are excellent, but porcelain is typically preferred for areas requiring extra strength and water resistance, while ceramic offers a cost-effective, flexible solution for most other spaces. We’re happy to help you choose the best option for your specific needs!

What’s the best type of tile for bathroom floors?

The best type of tile for bathroom floors is typically porcelain or ceramic tile, as both are highly water-resistant, durable, and easy to clean, which is essential in moisture-prone areas like bathrooms. How long does tile installation typically take?

The length of time for tile installation depends on the size of the project, the type of tile, and the condition of the surface. For a standard kitchen or bathroom floor, the process typically takes 2 to 3 days. Day one involves surface preparation, such as leveling, cleaning, or removing old flooring. Once the surface is ready, the actual tile installation happens on the second day, where the tiles are laid and aligned precisely.

After the tiles are set, grouting and finishing touches are done, which may take another day, depending on the size of the area and the type of grout used. In some cases, additional time may be needed for sealing the tiles, especially if using natural stone or porous materials.

Larger projects or complex designs, like custom layouts or backsplashes, may take longer. Overall, we ensure every step is done with care to deliver a high-quality, long-lasting tile installation that fits your schedule and needs.
